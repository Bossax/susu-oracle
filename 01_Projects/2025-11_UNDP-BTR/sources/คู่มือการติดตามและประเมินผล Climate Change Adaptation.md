>- Present the current situation of M&E at the National and sectoral levels
>- Propose guidance on sectoral monitoring and evaluation frameworks


**Monitoring** implies a process-based measurement., benchmarking against objectives and planned resources. 
**Evaluation** implies efficiency and equity of adaptation effort. (sounds also like process-based?)

Adaptation areas
each area is set objective for M&E, M&E approach,

1. Planning
	- Objective: clear long-term and integrative plan
	- M&E approach: number of long-term plans, coverage of long-term plans, sectoral plans
2. Implementation
	- Objective: implementation of adaptation mechanisms to increase climate resilience
	- M&E approach
		- short-term: progress of adaptation project as planned
		- long-term: Effectiveness and efficiency of adaptation projects, sustainability, cross-sectoral impact, mitigation co-benefit
3. Outcome
		- Objective: sectoral and economic resilience
		- M&E approach: 
			- short-term: reduce climate vulnerability to climate variability a
			- long-term: increase adaptive capacity, increase resilience

### Annotation
1. Definition mismatch
	1. $Exposure_{CU} = Exposure_{ipcc} \times Hazard_{ipcc}$
	2. Resilience is defined as consisting of exposure, adaptive capacity and sensitivity. However, *sectoral resilience indicators* are randomly picked without substantiation.  
2. Project-level indicators should be process-, output-, and outcome-based.
	- The proposed text includes quantitative indicators, mostly the counts of hard structure
	- It also describes the strategy and approach to water security. However, these are hardly called indicators. 
3. Monitoring indicators focus on climate and hazard indicators. So it tracks risk in that sector, not risk which the project is tackling. It is an annual measurement. 
4. Evaluation indicators should be outcome-, and impact- based. 
	- It uses resilience indicator which seems like vulnerability. Is this not the same as the Climate Resilience Indicator defined previously?
	- How is the water security index defined? what does it tell?
