# Potential project directions
## 1st track CRI = Climate Risk Index

ex-post climate impact based on SENDAI frame work indicators. for example,
- number of affected people
- number of affected agricultural land
- disaster relief payment

The data is at provincial level. The weighting scheme is equal weighting. Data is from national census database.

The result yields different spatial pattern as compared to the **spatial risk map.**

While the general trend is in agreement. e. g. the northeastern region has suffered the most economic damage and casualty, the results at the provincial level disagree. For example, the provinces that faces the highest risk and suffers the most impact are different provinces.

### Question
1. How to interpret this disparity?
2. is the regional similarity result from methodology similarity or result convergence?
	- the spatial risk map does include an overlapping set of indicators with the CRI
	- CRI could be biased due to data availability. Because CRI roughly separates economic damage aggregation by agricultural zone and non-agricultural zone, the data incompleteness of industrial and urban areas damage may skew the result.

### Possibility of project value added

1. Address the interpretability and synergy with the spatial risk map
2. Dasymetization methodology to improve data resolution
