### **Part 1: Introduction & Guidance Overview**

- **Slide 1-4: Introduction**
    
    - Overview of the Land Sector Emissions and Removals accounting guidance.
        
- **Slide 5-6: Standards & Guidance**
    
    - References the GHG Protocol Land Sector and Removals Guidance and the FLAG (Forest, Land, and Agriculture) Science Based Target Setting Guidance.
        
- **Slide 7: Guidance Structure**
    
    - Lists key components: Removal/Emission accounting, Land Use Change (LUC), Land Management, Target Setting, and Tracking Metrics.
        
- **Slide 8: Carbon Pools**
    
    - Defines the main carbon pools: Land-based (Biomass, Soil, Dead Organic Matter), Product, and Geologic .
        
- **Slide 9: Land Emissions Overview**
    
    - Illustrates fluxes between pools (Atmospheric, Land, Product) and categorizes activities like harvest, disturbance, and land preparation .
        
- **Slide 10: Land Tracking & Categories**
    
    - Breakdown of emissions vs. removals across Land Use Change, Land Management, and Product/Geologic pools .
        
- **Slide 11: Traceability & Data Tiers**
    
    - Explains that traceability (from jurisdiction down to specific farm) determines the quality of data (primary vs. secondary) .
        
- **Slide 12: Removal Accounting Requirements**
    
    - Lists requirements for claiming removals: full traceability, primary data usage, uncertainty quantification, and ongoing monitoring for reversals .
        
- **Slide 13: Activity-Based Data Minimums**
    
    - Sets expectations for data granularity (farm-level vs. spend-based) for Scope 1 and Scope 3 .
        
- **Slide 14: Stock-Change vs. Flow Accounting**
    
    - Contrasts measuring actual stock changes (Stock Difference) vs. estimating flows (Flow Accounting) .
        

### **Part 2: Land Use Change (LUC)**

- **Slide 15-16: Land Use Categories**
    
    - Lists standard categories: Forest, Grassland, Cropland, Wetlands, Settlements, and Other lands .
        
- **Slide 17: LUC Matrix**
    
    - Matrix showing conversion types (e.g., Forest to Crop = Emission; Crop to Forest = Removal) .
        
- **Slide 18: Quantifying LUC Emissions**
    
    - Differentiates between Direct LUC (dLUC) for owned/traceable lands and Statistical LUC (sLUC) for sourced products with lower traceability .
        
- **Slide 19: LUC Equations**
    
    - Presents formulas for calculating dLUC and sLUC emissions based on area, emission factors, and allocation factors .
        
- **Slide 20: Spatial Boundary & Traceability**
    
    - Links traceability levels (Jurisdictional, Sourcing Region, Land Management Unit) to the appropriate accounting metric (sLUC vs. dLUC) .
        
- **Slide 21: Allocation Methods**
    
    - Introduces methods to allocate emissions across multiple products (Economic, Mass) and across time (Linear, Equal discounting) .
        
- **Slide 22: Time Discounting**
    
    - Explains distributing LUC emissions over a 20-year assessment period (or harvest cycle if longer) .
        
- **Slide 23: Economic Allocation (Multi-product)**
    
    - Discusses allocating emissions based on revenue contribution (e.g., Timber vs. Corn vs. Coffee) but notes limitations like price volatility .
        
- **Slide 24: Crop Rotation Allocation**
    
    - Example of allocating emissions across time for crops with variable production cycles (Timber, Corn, Coffee) .
        
- **Slide 25: sLUC Allocation Factors**
    
    - Details "Shared Responsibility" (allocating by harvested area share) and "Production Expansion" (allocating by area change share) .
        
- **Slide 26: 5-Step Quantification Process**
    
    - Step-by-step guide: 1. Identify units/areas, 2. Detect LUC events, 3. Calculate emissions, 4. Time allocation, 5. Product allocation .
        
- **Slide 27-28: Certification Programs**
    
    - Discusses using certifications (e.g., RSB, SBP) to improve traceability and ensure Chain of Custody (Segregation vs. Mass Balance) .
        
- **Slide 29: Calculation Example**
    
    - Walkthrough of a company sourcing Barley (sLUC) and Cassava (dLUC) involving time discounting and product allocation .
        

### **Part 3: Land Tracking Metrics**

- **Slide 30-31: Purpose of Tracking Metrics**
    
    - Explains the need to track broader impacts (leakage) where local yield increases might inadvertently drive deforestation elsewhere .
        
- **Slide 32: Metric 1 - Indirect Land Use Change (iLUC)**
    
    - Accounts for market-mediated carbon stock losses using global or national factors .
        
- **Slide 33: Metric 2 - Carbon Opportunity Cost (COC)**
    
    - Compares current carbon storage against potential storage if land reverted to native vegetation .
        
- **Slide 34: Metric 3 - Land Occupation**
    
    - A simplified metric measuring the hectares required to produce a specific quantity of product .
        

### **Part 4: Land Management**

- **Slide 35-36: Land Management Overview**
    
    - Covers emissions from land remaining in the same use (e.g., fertilizer, livestock, soil carbon changes) .
        
- **Slide 37: Managed Land Proxy**
    
    - Allows companies to distinguish "managed" land (anthropogenic impact) from "unmanaged" land .
        
- **Slide 38: Methodological Tiers**
    
    - Reviews IPCC Tiers 1-3 for estimating stock changes (Activity-based, Remote sensing, Modeling, Measurement) .
        
- **Slide 39: "No Change" Assumption**
    
    - Criteria for assuming zero stock change (e.g., no land use change or practice change in 20 years) .
        
- **Slide 40: Activity-Based Approach**
    
    - Using standard emission factors for biomass and soil based on practices (tillage, inputs), noted as unable to estimate Dead Organic Matter (DOM) .
        
- **Slide 41: Other Approaches**
    
    - Brief mention of Remote Sensing, Modeling, and Direct Measurement as higher-tier options .
        
- **Slide 42: Non-CO2 Emissions**
    
    - Lists sources like enteric fermentation, manure, and biomass burning, linking to the Cool Farm Tool for calculation .
        
- **Slide 43: Software Example**
    
    - Screenshot of Cool Farm Tool input/output for sugarcane .
        

### **Part 5: Product Carbon Pool**

- **Slide 44-46: Biogenic Product Storage**
    
    - Accounting for carbon stored in long-lived products (e.g., wood). Uses a decay model based on product half-life to calculate emissions over time .
        

### **Part 6: Quantification Tools**

- **Slide 47-49: Remote Sensing**
    
    - Overview of sensor types (Lidar, Radar, Optical) and their application in measuring biomass and land cover change .
        
- **Slide 50-51: Databases**
    
    - List of key databases for emission factors: IPCC, Ecoinvent, FAOSTAT, and others (Note: GHG Protocol is a framework, not a database) .
