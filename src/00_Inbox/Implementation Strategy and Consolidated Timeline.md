## 1. Workload Analysis 

>[!important] **Attention Director:** The workload for January and February is high because of the volume of complex analysis required. We are not just writing reports; we are building technical systems.

**A. CRDB/BTR Literature Review (Jan):**
- **Task:** Review climate risks across **6 NAP Sectors** (Water, Agriculture, Tourism, Health, Natural Resources, Human Settlement).
- **Analytical Load:** We must extract specific "impact chains" (Cause -> Effect) to build the data model. This is not a copy-paste job; it is a structural analysis of scientific texts.
    

**B. NFCS Mandate & Governance Review (Jan):**
- **Task:** Analyze the **Climate Change Act** and **TMD Master Plan** to define the legal boundary for climate services.
- **Analytical Load:** This requires interpreting legal text to design a governance flowchart that DPT and DDPM will accept.
    

**C. CRDB Data Landscape & NCAIF Design (Jan-Feb):**
- **Task:** (1) Document all existing DCCE data systems (Data Landscape) and (2) Draft the **National Climate Adaptation Information Framework (NCAIF)**.
- **Analytical Load:** NCAIF is the brain of the system. We have to invent a taxonomy that works for both scientists and policy-makers.

**D. BTR Task 3 Baseline Framework & DRS (Jan-Feb):**
- **Task:** Design the **Baseline Framework** and **Data Reference Sheet (DRS)**.
- **Analytical Load:** We must align this with the CU Research Team's manual while ensuring it fits the "official" data reality. This involves defining formulas, units, and data dictionaries for national reporting.

**C. The CRI Technical Modeling (Feb-Mar):**
- **Scope:** We enhance Climate Risk Index. We synthesis a new methodology for Climate 'Readiness Index'. 
- **Complexity:** This requires "Dasymetric Mapping" (complex spatial calculation), which involves coding, not just Excel work. The new methodology will needs to be establish by Feb 2026

>**Conclusion:** The volume of _new_ theoretical design in Jan-Feb is our biggest risk. We are building the engine while driving the car.
