\subsubsection{Tag: ACI}\label{tag-aci}

{\href{zotero://open/library/items/S9GK92JG?page=2&annotation=ZVYPSUI7}{{``This
index computes the difference in amplitude of adjacent time samples
(milliseconds) in each frequency bin, resulting in high values when
analyzing sounds characterized by inner variability over time (typically
biophonies) and low values in the presence of constant sounds (such as
cars or vessel transits). The ACI was here therefore used as an
indicator of biological sounds, in order to separate biological from
anthropogenic inputs into the soundscape''}}}
{({\href{zotero://select/library/items/N7UY2TS8}{Pieretti et al., 2017,
p. 14}})} ACI {[}f(frequency){]} increases with the degree of
time-variation of the amplitude . low ACI = monotoneous sound

{\href{zotero://open/library/items/S9GK92JG?page=2&annotation=BLHC5UDN}{{``we

Time series of both Power Spectral Density (PSD) and Acoustic Complexity

\textbf{Power Spectral Density (PSD)}:~

\begin{itemize}
\item
  PSD was calculated on \textbf{every one-minute acoustic file} by
  applying a series of short-term FFTs.
\item
  The \textbf{FFT size used for PSD was 2048 points}. {{39.063 Hz
  frequency resolution}}
\item
  PSD levels above 620 Hz were compiled into 312 Hz bins to match ACI
  settings and improve visibility for fish vocalizations at low
  frequencies.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Acoustic Complexity Index (ACI)}:

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  The study performed \textbf{two parallel ACI analyses} with different
  FFT setting
\item
  \textbf{ACI (FFT 2048)} was used for the \textbf{0--620 Hz frequency
  range}, primarily associated with fish vocalizations. This setting
  provided a higher resolution for low-frequency bands.
\item
  \textbf{ACI (FFT 256)} was used for \textbf{frequency bins above 620
  Hz}, primarily associated with snapping shrimp sounds. For this higher
  frequency band (620 Hz--40 kHz). The frequency resolution is
  \textbf{312 Hz}.
