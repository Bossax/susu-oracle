## Tag: definition

“academic literature, which describes success as adaptations that support reductions in risk and vulnerability without compromising sustainability.” (Bartelet et al., 2025, p. 1)

## Tag: methodology

“we explored how the risk, vulnerability, and sustainability outcomes that operators experienced one year after experiencing a severe climate disturbance (either coral bleaching or a cyclone) related to the types of adaptation they adopted in response to the disturbance” (Bartelet et al., 2025, p. 1)

(Bartelet et al., 2025, p. 2) Study design  
- **Sample Population and Size:** The researchers conducted **209 surveys** on **adaptive responses** of the representatives of reef tourism companies (operators) across 28 locations and eight countries in the Asia-Pacific.  
- **Data Type and Timeline**: The study utilized **recall data** from participants regarding conditions *one month* before and *one year after* a specific climate disturbance occurred,namely **coral bleaching or a cyclone**.  
- Adaptive Responses: A classification of **10 potential adaptive responses** (plus an "other" option) was identified through existing empirical evidence, expert consultation, and pilot interviews  

*A longitudinal study* is a research design that involves repeated observations of the same variables over long periods of time

## Tag: spatial diversification

“Spatial diversification Changing reef sites company was visiting on tours.” (Bartelet et al., 2025, p. 4)

“Our findings show that spatial diversification responses contributed to operator resilience to climate-related disturbances” (Bartelet et al., 2025, p. 9)

“Although spatial diversification helped to absorb disturbance (resilience), restoration-focused responses were associated with attempts to influence and manage resilience by reducing disturbance risks” (Bartelet et al., 2025, p. 9)

## Tag: important

“However, our findings indicate that at least some adaptation responses were effective in promoting desirable outcomes, such as reductions in risk and vulnerability.” (Bartelet et al., 2025, p. 1)

“Our findings suggest scientific needs for further research on the causal relationships between adaptation measures and their outcomes, experimentation with different statistical methods, and empirical tests of the generalizability of our findings in different contexts over space and time” (Bartelet et al., 2025, p. 1)

“We found that all dimensions of adaptation success could be measured and applied to a micro-level where we specifically focused on risk, vulnerability, and sustainability outcomes as perceived by an individual reef tourism operator” (Bartelet et al., 2025, p. 8)

“This indicates that the support-seeking response may be qualitatively different between bleaching and cyclones, more relief-based for cyclones and perhaps more focused on collective action for bleaching” (Bartelet et al., 2025, p. 9) ==expectation of government actions post disaster sets the expectation of operator's actions==

“This finding raises the question of whether diversifying in response to climate change will necessarily be associated with positive outcomes” (Bartelet et al., 2025, p. 9)

“Thus, from a public policy perspective, a better understanding of the strengths and weaknesses of different kinds of response diversity would support targeted financial support that provides a greater return on investment” (Bartelet et al., 2025, p. 9)

“Our findings show that spatial diversification responses contributed to operator resilience to climate-related disturbances” (Bartelet et al., 2025, p. 9)
