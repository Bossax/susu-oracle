# Executive Summary: The "Two-Track" Strategy

Meijiâ€™s concept is fundamentally a **"Split-Level" M&E System**. She is explicitly separating the **operational tracking** (Monitoring) from the **impact assessment** (Evaluation).

![[pic/creagy adaptaion M&E concept.jpg]]
### 1. Monitoring vs. Evaluation

Meiji proposes a hard line between two distinct data flows (Slide 14):

- **Track 1: Monitoring (The "Accountability" Loop)**
    - **Owner:** Line Agencies (Water, Agriculture, Tourism, etc.).
    - **Focus:** **Outputs** and **Activities**.
    - **Data:** Raw numbers from projects (e.g., "500 ponds built," "1,500 farmers trained").
    - **Purpose:** To verify that government budget was spent as planned.
    - **Destination:** DCCE (as a repository).
        
- **Track 2: Evaluation (The "Resilience" Loop)**
    
    - **Owner:** DCCE / NESDC / Budget Bureau.
    - **Focus:** **Outcomes** and **Impacts**.
    - **Data:** Synthesized indices (e.g., "Reduced economic loss," "Increased water security").
    - **Purpose:** To answer "Did we actually get safer?" and report to UNFCCC (BTR/NC).
    - **Mechanism:** Meiji explicitly notes that _Evaluation indicators require further study_ to translate the Monitoring data into higher-level answers.
        

This separation is smart because it frees line agencies from the burden of complex impact calculations. They just report "what they did" (Monitoring), and DCCE/Consultants handle the "so what?" (Evaluation).

### 2. Standard Indicator Subset

To make disparate projects comparable, she proposes a **"Standard Output Indicator"** system (Slides 21-25). Instead of every agency making up their own metrics, they must map their work to a code like:

- **E-Series (Exposure Reduction):** e.g., `E-1.1` Area protected (sq km).
- **S-Series (Sensitivity Reduction):** e.g., `S-1.1` Area with reduced dependency on sensitive resources.
- **A-Series (Adaptive Capacity):** e.g., `A-1.3` People trained.
