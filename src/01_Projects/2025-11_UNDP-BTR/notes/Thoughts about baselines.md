Often time, monitoring and evaluating adaptation effort is done through tracking indicators and metrics. Indicators are observables that could be qualitative and quantitative. Metrics have a more quantitative sense. Assessing baseline can mean simply picking a point value from an agreed base year. Some indicators are obvious; easily obtained through national census survey and consisting of only one variable. Some are not so simple, demanding multi-step calculation and a multitude of data sources across scales. 

Indicators types
1. **Output-based indicators** measure the **products and services delivered** as a direct result of activities within a development intervention.
2. **Outcome-based indicators** measure the **intended or achieved short-to-medium-term effects of an interventionâ€™s outputs**. They represent **changes in development conditions** that occur between the completion of outputs and the achievement of impact.
3. An **impact-based indicator** measures the **long-term societal, economic, or environmental consequences** resulting from a development intervention. These indicators are used to assess the effectiveness of adaptation actions.

One way to frame the baseline framework is to serve the establishment of the pre-condition before implementing adaptation projects. The information of the Baseline Assessment will be used in the "planning" stage where decisions on what strategies and measures would be implemented. It becomes relevant and crucial in the MEL stage.
