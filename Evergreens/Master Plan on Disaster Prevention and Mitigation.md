# Overview of Thailand's National Disaster Prevention and Mitigation Plan (2021–2027)

The **National Disaster Prevention and Mitigation Plan B.E. 2564–2570 (2021–2027)** serves as Thailand's master plan for managing disaster risks. It aims to build a **resilient nation** through the principles of **"knowing the risks, adapting, recovering quickly and comprehensively, and sustainably."** The plan is aligned with domestic and international frameworks, such as the **Sendai Framework**, **Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)**, and the **ASEAN Agreement on Disaster Management and Emergency Response (AADMER).**

---

# **Key Objectives**
1. Enhance sustainable recovery after disasters.
2. Reduce disaster risks and vulnerabilities.
3. Promote innovation and knowledge in disaster risk management.
4. Strengthen international partnerships for disaster response.

---

# **Key Strategies**
#### 1. Enhancing Sustainable Recovery Efficiency
- Develop a **risk assessment system** for post-disaster recovery to "build back better."
- Create recovery management guidelines.
- Strengthen recovery approaches to ensure safety, resilience, and inclusivity (e.g., prioritizing vulnerable groups).

#### 2. Improving Disaster Risk Management Systems
- Promote community-based disaster risk management (**CBDRM**).
- Develop smart communities/cities capable of adapting to disasters.
- Apply innovative technologies for better disaster management.

#### 3. Developing Disaster Risk Management Knowledge
- Establish a national disaster information platform.
- Foster collaboration at local and international levels.
- Certify disaster management experts at an international standard.

#### 4. Enhancing Disaster Warning Systems
- Implement multi-hazard early warning systems.
- Provide timely, accurate warnings to communities in disaster-prone areas.

#### 5. Promoting Participatory Investment in Disaster Risk Management
- Encourage investment from government, private sectors, and civil society.
- Build capacity for "smart" communities and cities aligned with resilience principles.

#### 6. Strengthening International Partnerships
- Collaborate with ASEAN, Mekong River Commission, and other organizations for humanitarian assistance.
- Exchange best practices for sustainable development.

#### 7. Developing a Unified Humanitarian Assistance Coordination System
- Improve coordination between national agencies, international organizations, and civil society.
- Train personnel in data management and analysis for effective disaster response.

---
# **Underlying Principles

1. **Resilience:** Build a nation capable of adapting to risks and recovering sustainably.
2. **Build Back Better and Safer:** Reduce vulnerabilities during reconstruction to prevent future risks.
3. **Risk-Informed Approach:** Use risk assessments to guide decisions and investments.
4. **Comprehensive DRM Cycle:** Address prevention, preparedness, response, and recovery holistically.
5. **Whole-of-Government & Whole-of-Society Approach:** Involve all sectors in disaster risk reduction efforts.
6. **Integration with National & International Frameworks:** Align with Thailand’s National Strategy (2018–2037), Sufficiency Economy Philosophy (SEP), SDGs, Sendai Framework, etc.
7. **Smart DRM:** Leverage digital tools and innovation to enhance efficiency.

---

# **Operational Aspects**
#### Risk Assessment
- Develop risk maps using local wisdom and scientific data.
#### Risk Reduction Measures
- Implement strategies like relocation of settlements, ecosystem-based solutions, insurance mechanisms, and preparedness plans.
#### Early Warning Systems
- Use end-to-end systems for timely alerts via multiple communication channels.
#### Emergency Response
- Adopt the Incident Command System (**ICS**) for unified emergency management.
#### Damage Assessment & Post-Recovery Needs
- Conduct detailed assessments to inform both immediate relief efforts and long-term recovery plans.
#### Financial Mechanisms
- Use contingency funds for immediate relief and integrate budgets for disaster risk management.

---
# **Scope of Disasters**
The plan addresses various types of disasters:
- Natural: Floods, storms, droughts, earthquakes, tsunamis, landslides.
- Environmental: Wildfires, haze pollution (PM2.5), climate change impacts.
- Structural: Building collapses.
- Security-related: Threats with national implications.


# Relevant agencies and their roles

**1. National Level Coordinating Body:**
- **คณะกรรมการป้องกันและบรรเทาสาธารณภัยแห่งชาติ (กปภ.ช.) (National Disaster Prevention and Mitigation Committee)**: This committee holds the primary responsibility for setting national policies on disaster prevention and mitigation. It also plays a crucial role in integrating disaster prevention and mitigation systems across various government agencies, local administrative organizations, and other relevant entities.

**2. Key Government Ministries and Their Roles:**
- **กระทรวงมหาดไทย (Ministry of Interior)**: The Department of Disaster Prevention and Mitigation (กรมป้องกันและบรรเทาสาธารณภัย) under the Ministry of Interior appears to be a central operational agency.
    - The Director-General under the Disaster Prevention and Mitigation Act B.E. 2550 (2007) serves as the Incident Commander at various levels (province, district, local administrative organization).
    - The Ministry is the primary policy and operational focal point for the Urban Search and Rescue (USAR) team and is responsible for coordinating with relevant agencies to enhance its capabilities.
    - The Ministry, along with the Ministry of Finance and the National Economic and Social Development Council, is involved in risk avoidance measures such as land-use planning and relocating communities from disaster-prone areas.
    - It is also a key agency in risk prevention and mitigation, responsible for enforcing building standards and safety regulations.
    - The Ministry plays a significant role in preparedness by organizing disaster prevention and mitigation drills at all levels and preparing evacuation plans and temporary shelters.
    - During emergency response, the Ministry, through the Department of Disaster Prevention and Mitigation, is central to command and control, and coordination with other agencies.
    - The Ministry is also involved in damage assessment and needs analysis (DANA) and post-disaster needs assessment (PDNA).
    
- **กระทรวงการต่างประเทศ (Ministry of Foreign Affairs)**: This ministry is responsible for international cooperation in disaster risk management.
    - It coordinates and collaborates with Thai government agencies, diplomatic missions, and international organizations in Thailand to provide relief and assistance to foreign victims.
    - The ministry also coordinates, cooperates with, and facilitates assistance from foreign countries and international organizations for disaster risk management efforts.
    - It engages in coordination and collaboration with foreign entities on all aspects of disaster risk management, including disaster risk reduction, prevention, preparedness, emergency response, recovery, build back better, and post-disaster needs assessment.
    - The ministry disseminates information about Thailand's role in providing and receiving aid and managing disasters to foreign media and international organizations.
    - The Ministry, along with the Office of the National Economic and Social Development Council, supports the implementation of the United Nations Sustainable Development Cooperation Framework (UNSDCF) and collaborates with the United Nations Country Team (UNCT) on development initiatives, including disaster risk management and climate change adaptation with UNDP and UNDRR.
    - The Ministry is also involved in promoting Thailand's role as an emerging donor through South-South Cooperation.

- **กระทรวงสาธารณสุข (Ministry of Public Health)**: This ministry is the lead agency for medical and public health emergency response.
    - It develops policies, strategies, and integrated plans for medical and public health emergency management in collaboration with relevant networks.
    - The ministry leads the medical and public health response during emergencies and disasters, operating an incident command system for medical and public health, directing its agencies, and coordinating with public and private sector partners.
    - It develops surveillance, risk assessment, disease and health hazard forecasting, and warning systems in coordination with relevant agencies.
    - The ministry operates Public Health Emergency Operation Centers (PHEOC) at various levels for coordination.
    - It ensures the preparedness of hospitals at all levels, trains healthcare personnel and volunteers, and provides medical supplies.
    - The ministry develops emergency medical teams (EMT) at various levels, including the Thailand EMT for national and international deployment.
    - It establishes and supports emergency care systems, including pre-hospital (EMS), in-hospital, and inter-hospital care.
    - The ministry also develops communication systems for its network, both domestically and internationally.
    - It is responsible for monitoring, controlling, and tracking communicable diseases and outbreaks among disaster victims and providing medical care, sanitation, and disease prevention.
    
- **กระทรวงการคลัง (Ministry of Finance)**: This ministry is involved in financial aspects of disaster risk management.
    - It manages the central budget for disaster prevention and mitigation.
    - The ministry sets regulations for the use of the government's reserve fund for emergency disaster relief.
    - The Ministry, along with the Ministry of Interior, is involved in financial risk transfer mechanisms such as developing insurance systems and issuing catastrophe bonds.
- **สำนักงานสภาพัฒนาการเศรษฐกิจและสังคมแห่งชาติ (Office of the National Economic and Social Development Council - NESDC)**:
    - The NESDC's involvement is highlighted in the context of Thailand being ranked as the 8th country most at risk from climate change impacts, with reference to their 2011 report on the significant economic damage from the major flood that year.
    - The NESDC, along with the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, supports the implementation of the UNSDCF.
    
- **กระทรวงกลาโหม (Ministry of Defence)**: The military plays a supportive role in humanitarian assistance.

- **กรมประชาสัมพันธ์ (Public Relations Department)**: This department collaborates with the Department of Disaster Prevention and Mitigation to develop guidelines for disseminating information during emergencies and to establish Joint Information Centers (JIC).

- **กระทรวงการพัฒนาสังคมและความมั่นคงของมนุษย์ (Ministry of Social Development and Human Security)**: This ministry is likely involved in addressing the vulnerabilities of specific groups and providing social support to disaster victims, although the excerpts provide limited specific details on their operational roles.

**3. Other Relevant Organizations and Frameworks:**

- **องค์กรปกครองส่วนท้องถิ่น (Local Administrative Organizations)**: These organizations have a crucial role in implementing the national disaster prevention and mitigation plan at the local level and are responsible for preparing and responding to disasters within their jurisdiction. They are also expected to have their own disaster prevention and mitigation plans aligned with the national plan.

- **ภาคเอกชน (Private Sector)**: Businesses and financial institutions are encouraged to integrate disaster risk reduction into their operations and engage in Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) initiatives related to disaster preparedness and resilience. They are also mentioned as potential investors in disaster risk management.

- **ภาคประชาสังคม (Civil Society)**: Non-profit organizations play a role in disaster risk management and are encouraged to participate in risk reduction efforts. They are also key partners in humanitarian assistance.

- **ศูนย์ประสานงานการให้ความช่วยเหลือด้านมนุษยธรรมของอาเซียน (ASEAN Coordinating Centre for Humanitarian Assistance on disaster management - AHA Centre)**: Thailand supports the operations of the AHA Centre.

- **โครงการพัฒนาแห่งสหประชาชาติ (United Nations Development Programme - UNDP)** and **สำนักงานเพื่อการลดความเสี่ยงจากภัยพิบัติแห่งสหประชาชาติ (United Nations Office for Disaster Risk Reduction - UNDRR)**: These UN agencies collaborate with Thailand on disaster risk management initiatives and the implementation of international frameworks like the Sendai Framework and SDGs.

- **ศูนย์ข้อมูลประชาสัมพันธ์ร่วม (Joint Information Center - JIC)**: This is a crucial structure at all levels of emergency operations, serving as a central point for disseminating accurate and timely information to the public, media, and relevant agencies.
