Purpose: To capture, codify, and manage unwritten institutional realities (political, operational, and behavioral) that impact project delivery.

# 1. Institutional & Political 

_Focus: Budgeting, Mandates, and Strategic Positioning_

| **Insight (Tacit Knowledge)**          | **Context & Implication**                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             | **Strategic Action**                                                                                                                                                                              |
| -------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Service vs. Infrastructure Dilemma** | The Ministry of Digital Economy classifies "Climate Services" as a soft service rather than "Digital Infrastructure." Consequently, TMD struggles to request implementation budgets for projects that are not deemed service related such as pioneering new products. | **Re-framing Strategy:** The Costed Action Plan (WP3) must frame climate services as "Technical Infrastructure" or "Capacity Development" to align with eligible budget codes.                    |
| **Strategic Vacuum**                   | TMD currently lacks a proactive climate change strategy. The agenda is often reactive rather than setting the national direction.                                                                                                                                     | **De Facto Strategy:** The NFCS Roadmap we produce will likely serve as the _de facto_ internal strategy. We must ensure it is robust enough to stand alone without a parent strategy to lean on. |
| **Division vs. Department**            | Climate services are currently viewed as the work of the "Climatology Division" rather than a whole-of-department mission. This limits cross-departmental buy-in.                                                                                                     | **Elevation:** The governance structure must explicitly involve higher-level Departmental executives to shift ownership from "Division-level" to "Department-level."                              |

>[!note]
>- [ ] Compare TMD with GISTDA. GISTDA is also an organization that aims to provide information services just like TMD. However, GISTDA manages to acquire a lot of budget to implement proactive information service projects. 
# 2. Operational

_Focus: Internal Capacity and Workflow_

| **Insight (Tacit Knowledge)**                                                              | **Context & Implication**                                                                                                                                                                                             | **Strategic Action**                                                                                                                                                        |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Task Force Fatigue**                                                                     | TMD typically assigns existing project staff to Task Forces, leading to overburdened members and low engagement.                                                                                                      | **Synergy (Piggybacking):** Do not create a new standalone Task Force. Leverage the upcoming "Urban Act" Task Force to reduce administrative burden and ensure continuity . |

All team members have to be involved in every process in the project cycle including technical staff involved in site visit and stakeholder engagement. |                                                                                                                                                                             |

_Focus: User Behavior and "The Gap"_

| ----------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **The "Tokenism" Risk**       | Agencies often send "the available person" rather than "the right person" to workshops. Non-technical attendees cannot contribute meaningfully to co-creation. | **Strict Profiling:** Develop an "Engagement Playbook" that defines specific technical requirements for nominees (e.g., "Must be a hydrologist" or "Must manage urban drainage data"). |
